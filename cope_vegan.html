    • Vegan in Copenhagen? I've got you covered

    Copenhagen, Denmark is one of my favorite cities in the world. I was lucky enough to live in the city for four months last year, and even though I wasn't vegan during my stay, I ate a TON of delicious vegan food.

    Not only is Copenhagen a capital of healthy, organic food, but it's also a great place to try plant-based products. Here is a list of some of my favorite vegan restaurants and food spots that I found around the city:

    42 Raw

    This cute little spot is the perfect place to start a day in the city. Try their Chia Pudding bowls, Acai Bowls, or Matcha Bowls for a healthy breakfast, or grab one of their homemade energy bites from the front counter. All of the food here is seriously Instagrammable, so get ready to take lots of pictures.

    Made in Italy

    This delicious vegan pizza is a part of Copenhagen Street Food, a long-term food truck event in a famous Christianshavn warehouse nicknamed, PapirØen, or "Paper Island". At the "Made in Italy" stand, you have the choice of tons of cheeseless pizzas. The one I tried had a bomb cashew sauce that doesn't let you miss cheese a bit.

    DØP

    If you've never had a Danish hot dog before, you're missing out. The Danes don't mess around with toppings, and lucky for vegetarians and vegans, you can still try the traditional street food at DØP (Den Økologiske Pølsemand). This hot dog truck is located right next to the famous "Round Tower" in the central city and offers all kinds of sausage options, including a vegan option. Make sure you order your "pølse" with mustard, pickles, ketchup, fresh onions and fried onions.

    Grød

    Grød is hands down, my favorite restaurant in Copenhagen. Not only is is affordable (relative to everything else in this city), but everything on the menu is a form of "porridge". There's everything from savory risotto to oatmeal to chia pudding to choose from, and many of the menu items can be made vegan with almond milk. With so many toppings to choose from, you'll never go back to normal oatmeal again.

    Side note: this is the place that inspired my favorite breakfast ever...
