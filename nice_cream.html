    • How to Make Healthy Vegan Ice Cream

    Ice cream is life. When I was a kid I ate it every night after dinner while watching some super wholesome DVD box set, like "I Love Lucy" or "The Brady Bunch". This went on all the way through high school until my adult metabolism kicked in and I discovered that eating extensive amounts of ice cream wasn't the healthiest thing to do.

    Luckily, I discovered this amazing thing called "Nice Cream". Because it's made out of fruit and has no added sugar or dairy products, it's 100 percent vegan and so healthy, you could actually eat it for breakfast. The best part: it actually tastes like ice cream.

    Another amazing thing about this kind of ice cream is how easy it is to make. Pretty much all you need is a blender and some frozen fruit, plus whatever add-ins you'd like. It's just like making a smoothie, but better.

    The next two recipes are some of my favorites, but you can seriously mix in whatever you want. Using the same kind of blending method and fruit-to-liquid ratio is all you need.

    This one goes out to all of you that are addicted to ice cream but can't afford vegan Ben and Jerry's. I got you.

    Two Healthy Ice Cream Recipes

    Peanut Butter, Cinnamon and Banana Ice Cream

    Serves one hungry b*tch

    Ingredients

    • 1 frozen and sliced banana
    • 1/2 tsp. cinnamon
    • 1 tbsp. Powdered or 1/2 tbsp. regular peanut butter
    • 2 tbsp. almond milk
    • Cashews (optional)

    Instructions

    1. Place frozen bananas and cinnamon in a food processor or high-powered blender in
    2. Blend for one minute, or until smooth
    3. Add almond milk and blend again for a few seconds for a smoother texture
    4. Add chopped cashews to top

    Coconut Mango Sorbet

    Ingredients

    • 1 cup frozen mango pieces
    • 3 tbsp. coconut milk
    • Coconut Flakes (optional)
    • Sliced Strawberries (optional)

    Instructions

    1. Place mango pieces in a food processor or high-powered blender
    2. Blend for one minute, or until smooth
    3. Add coconut milk for an "ice cream" texture.
    4. Add coconut flakes and strawberries to top
